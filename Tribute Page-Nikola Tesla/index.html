Nikola Tesla

A man from the future

Nikola Tesla and his project Tesla coil, device that can transmit electricity wirelessly

Here are some of the the life events of the scientist Nikola Tesla in chronological order:

  • 1856 - Smilijan, Croatia
  • 1875 - Enrolled at the Austria Polytechnic in Graz to study electrical engineering
  • 1878-1882 - Worked in various electrical engineering positions in Europe
  • 1884 - Immigrated to the US and began working with Thomas Edition
  • 1885 - Resigned from Edison's company due to differences in their approaches to electrical engineering
  • 1887 - Established the Tesla Electric Company and began working on various inventions, including motor and transformer
  • 1888 - Developed the induction motor and alternating circuit(AC) power transmission system
  • 1888-1889 - Demonstrated his AC system, catching the attention of George Westinghouse, who later adopted it for power distribution
  • 1891 - The Westinghouse Electric Company began building the first hydroelectric power plant at Niagara Falls, using Tesla's AC system
  • 1893 - Tesla's inventions and patents gained widespread recognition and use, solidifying AC power as the standard for electrical power transmission
  • 1895 - Tesla's New York City laboratory was destroyed in a fire, and he suffered significant financial setbacks
  • 1899 - Moved to Colorado Springs, where he conducted experiments on wireless transmission of power and studied atmospheric electricity
  • 1900 - Returned to New York and began working on his Wardenclyffe Tower project, aiming to establish wireless communication and transmit power wirelessly
  • 1905 - The Wardenclyffe Tower project faced financial difficulties and was eventually abandoned
  • 1915 - Tesla filed for bankruptcy, facing financial challenges and declining health
  • 1928 - Received his last patent for a biplane capable of vertical takeoff and landing
  • 1943 - Nikola Tesla passed away in New York City at the age of 86
